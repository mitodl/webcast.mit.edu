THE CONFERENCE

 

The Brazil Conference has two main goals.

First, it creates an academic and politically neutral environment to discuss controversial issues in a context of increasing polarization in Brazil. We strive to go beyond easy and commonsensical answers.

Second, it is a platform to bring together people from all over Brazil and from different working fields in order to debate and empower transformative actions. The Brazil Conference is divided into four main pillars, described below. Each pillar in the conference is divided into three panels, which comprise a different approach to what we consider the main pillars of the Brazilian development.

Each pillar in the conference is divided into three panels, which comprise a different approach to what we consider the main pillars of the Brazilian development. Speakers frame the pillar topic casting the main challenges and opportunities in the different areas of the conference.

In these panels people with different perspectives and institutional background will debate the challenges for Brazilian development. Practitioners engaged in cutting-edge projects will present how their actions will build the country's future.
