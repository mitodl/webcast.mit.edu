About Mayor Rahm Emanuel
Mayor Emanuel has served since 2011 as the 55th mayor of Chicago. To help secure the city's future, his administration has invested in infrastructure, public transportation, open space and culture, while attracting major corporations and fostering a new generation of start-ups. Previously, Mayor Emanuel served as chief of staff to President Barack Obama and represented Chicago's 5th District in the US House of Representatives for three terms. From 1993 to 1998, he also served as a key advisor during the Clinton administration, rising to Senior Advisor to the President for Policy and Strategy.
